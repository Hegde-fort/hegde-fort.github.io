abstract: "We characterize the sample complexity of PAC learning under fairness constraints, showing that demographic parity and equalized odds each require Θ(d/ε² + log(1/δ)/ε²) samples — matching the unconstrained rate up to constants. Our proof introduces a new notion of fairness-aware Rademacher complexity. We also show that intersectional fairness constraints over k groups incur an unavoidable Θ(k) overhead that cannot be removed without additional structural assumptions."
