abstract: "We address differentially private stochastic bandits. We present two (near)-optimal Thompson Sampling-based learning algorithms: DP-TS and Lazy-DP-TS. The core idea in achieving optimality is the principle of optimism in the face of uncertainty. We reshape the posterior distribution in an optimistic way as compared to non-private Thompson Sampling. DP-TS achieves a near-optimal problem-dependent regret bound, and Lazy-DP-TS gets rid of an extra log factor by using the idea of dropping observations, matching the regret lower bound. We also conduct experiments to compare the empirical performance of our proposed algorithms with existing optimal algorithms for differentially private stochastic bandits."
